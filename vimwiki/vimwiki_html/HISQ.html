HISQ

  • It stands for Highly Improved Staggered Action
  • HISQ fermions utilize two levels of smearing. The first level treatment is: \[ \begin{eqnarray} c_{1}=\frac{1}{8} \\ c_{3}=\frac{1}{16} \\ c_{5}=\frac{1}{64} \\ c_{7}=\frac{1}{384} \\ \end{eqnarray} \] where \(c_{i}\) is coefficient of i-link staples. {where i=1,3,5,7} i.e. (coefficient of 1-link, 3-link, 5-link, 7-link staples)

The first-level smeared link is then projected back to U(3) before the application of the second level smearing. The second level uses: \[ \begin{align*} c_{3}=\frac{1}{16}\\ c_{5}=\frac{1}{64}\\ c_{7}=\frac{1}{384}\\ c_{Lepage}=\frac{1}{8}\\ c_{Naik}=\frac{-1}{24}+ \frac{\epsilon}{8}\\ \end{align*} .\]

where \(c_{Lepage}\) and \(c_{Naik}\) are coefficient for Lepage and Naik terms. We have used HISQ action which is tree-level improved Luscher-weisz action in gauge sector. The relative weights of plaquette and rectangle terms are: \[ c_{plaq}=\frac{5}{3} c_{rect}=\frac{-1}{12} \] The value of parameter in the force cut-off that we used in force filter is \(\delta=10^{-5}\), which shall be tuned for calculations near chiral limit.
